 SECTION 1  —  DAILY TIMELINE (do these in order every day)
===============================================================================

------------------------------------------------------------------------------
 STEP 1  —  BEFORE 9:15 AM  (Pre-market prep)
------------------------------------------------------------------------------
 1. Open the NSE Heatmap (or TradingView Stock Heatmap: NIFTY 50 / NIFTY 500,
    colored by % change).
 2. Find the 1-2 STRONGEST GREEN SECTORS (e.g. IT, Banking, Auto).
    - A strong stock in a weak sector often fails. Sector strength matters.
 3. Inside those sectors, shortlist the 2-4 MOST BULLISH stocks
    (biggest green move + high volume).
 4. Check NIFTY itself:
    - NIFTY green / above yesterday's close  -> favor BUY setups.
    - NIFTY red / falling                    -> favor SELL setups or trade nothing.
    (The script's NIFTY filter enforces this automatically.)
 5. Add your shortlist to a TradingView watchlist.
    Apply the V6 strategy to each chart:
       - Timeframe = 5 minute
       - Timezone  = Asia/Kolkata  (right-click time axis -> Timezone -> Kolkata)

 RULE: Don't marry one stock. Pick 3-4, let the STRATEGY decide which triggers.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------
 STEP 2  —  9:15 to 9:30 AM  (Watch only, DO NOT trade)
------------------------------------------------------------------------------
 - This is the OPENING RANGE = the grey shaded band on the chart.
 - NO TRADES here. You are only letting the OR High / OR Low lines form.
 - Gap check: if a stock opened more than ~2% above/below yesterday's close,
   the script blocks it (gap guard). Skip it - breakouts fail after big gaps.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------
 STEP 3  —  AFTER 9:30 AM  (Hunt for the signal)
------------------------------------------------------------------------------
 Take a trade ONLY when the script prints a  BUY (green)  or  SELL (red)  label.
 That label fires only when ALL of this is true on a CLOSED 5-min candle:

   1. Price crosses above OR High (BUY) / below OR Low (SELL)  = breakout candle
   2. Score >= 100 / 120   = at least 5 of 6 quality filters agree
   3. Time is within 9:30 - 14:30
   4. Not lunch time (12:00 - 13:00 is skipped)
   5. Fewer than 2 trades used today
   6. Daily loss/profit limit not yet hit

 The 6 filters inside the SCORE (each worth 20 points):
   [ ] Price above VWAP
   [ ] EMA20 > EMA50 (trend up)
   [ ] ADX > 20            (real trend, not sideways chop)
   [ ] RVOL > 1.5          (volume surge vs average)
   [ ] NIFTY trending your direction
   [ ] Stock stronger than NIFTY (relative strength)

 RULE: No arrow = the setup isn't good enough. Do NOT force a trade.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------
 STEP 4  —  WHEN THE ARROW APPEARS  (Log the paper trade)
------------------------------------------------------------------------------
 Read the TRADE TICKET panel (bottom-right of chart) and write it down EXACTLY:

       Stock   : INFY
       Side    : BUY
       Entry   : 1624.00      <- your entry price
       Stop    : 1617.00      <- exit here if wrong
       Target  : 1638.00      <- exit here if right (2R)
       Qty     : 71           <- position size (shares)
       Risk    : Rs 497       <- maximum you can lose on this trade
       Score   : 110/120
       Time    : 9:45

 That is your entire trade. Every number is given - nothing to calculate.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------
 STEP 5  —  MANAGING THE TRADE  (hands off)
------------------------------------------------------------------------------
 - Do NOTHING until price hits either the Stop or the Target.
 - Hits TARGET  -> WIN  (+2R, about +Rs 1000 on Rs 500 risk). Log it.
 - Hits STOP    -> LOSS (-1R, about -Rs 500). Log it. Losses are normal.
 - NEVER widen your stop to avoid a loss. That is the #1 way to blow up.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------
 STEP 6  —  EXITS & DAILY STOP RULES
------------------------------------------------------------------------------
 - Max 2 trades per day. After 2 entries you are DONE for the day.
 - Daily loss limit  -Rs 2000  -> stop trading, walk away.
 - Daily profit limit +Rs 3000 -> stop trading, protect the win.
 - No NEW trades after 14:30.
 - Force-exit any open trade at 15:20 (never hold into the close).

===============================================================================
 SECTION 3  —  HOW POSITION SIZE IS CALCULATED (so you understand the Qty)
===============================================================================
 Risk per share = Entry - Stop            (Stop = Entry - ATR x 1.5, tick-rounded)
 Rupees to risk = Capital x Risk %         (e.g. 100000 x 0.5% = Rs 500)
 Quantity       = floor(Rupees to risk / Risk per share)
 Example        : floor(500 / 7.00) = 71 shares ;  Total risk = 71 x 7 = Rs 497

 Set your Capital and Risk % in:
   Strategy Settings -> Inputs -> "Position Sizing (V7)"

 SECTION 4  —  HOW TO GET NOTIFICATIONS (TradingView Alerts)
===============================================================================
 The script already sends a full alert (Entry, Stop, Target, Qty, Risk, Score)
 every time a BUY/SELL fires. You just have to create ONE alert to receive them.

 ---- A. Install the mobile app (for phone push notifications) -----------------
 1. Install "TradingView" app on Android/iOS.
 2. Log in with the SAME account you use on the desktop/web.
 3. Allow notifications for the app in your phone settings.

 ---- B. Create the alert (do this on desktop/web) -----------------------------
 1. Open the 5-min chart of the stock and make sure "Momentum Trader Pro V6"
    is added to the chart.
 2. Click the ALARM CLOCK icon (right toolbar) OR press the "Alert" button,
    OR right-click the chart -> "Add alert".
 3. In the "Condition" dropdown (first row):
        - Select:  Momentum Trader Pro V6
        - Second dropdown: choose  "alert() function calls only"
          (this is what sends the ticket text - do NOT pick a plot condition)
 4. "Trigger" / frequency: set to  "Once Per Bar Close".
        (Matches the no-repaint design; alert fires on the confirmed candle.)
 5. Expiration: on paid plans set a far date; free plan alerts may expire.
 6. Go to the "Notifications" tab and enable what you want:
        [x] Notify on app        (phone push - needs the mobile app, step A)
        [x] Show pop-up          (desktop pop-up)
        [x] Play sound
        [x] Send email
        [ ] Webhook URL          (advanced - for auto-trading via a broker bridge)
 7. Click "Create".

 ---- C. What you receive ------------------------------------------------------
 When a signal fires you get a message like:

        BUY INFY
        Entry : 1624.00
        Stop  : 1617.00  (7.00/sh)
        Tgt   : 1638.00  (2.0R)
        Qty   : 71
        Risk  : Rs 497
        Score : 110/120

 ---- D. Important notes -------------------------------------------------------
 - You need ONE alert PER STOCK you want to watch. Watching 4 stocks = 4 alerts.
 - FREE TradingView plan = only 1-2 active alerts at a time. Paid plans allow
   many more. Plan your watchlist around your alert limit.
 - The chart/alert must stay running on TradingView's servers (once created,
   server-side alerts run even if your browser is closed - that's normal).
 - "Once Per Bar Close" means the alert arrives a few seconds AFTER the 5-min
   candle closes - this is correct and non-repainting. Do not switch to
   "Once Per Bar" (that can fire mid-candle and repaint).

 ---- E. (Optional) Auto-trading via Webhook -----------------------------------
 The Webhook option can POST the alert to a broker bridge (e.g. a service that
 connects to Zerodha/Dhan/etc.). This is advanced, needs a third-party bridge,
 and carries real execution risk. Do NOT enable until you have paper-traded
 successfully for weeks. Ask if you want this set up later.

 SECTION 5  —  PAPER-TRADING DISCIPLINE
===============================================================================
 1. Keep a journal (spreadsheet) with:
        Date | Stock | Side | Entry | Stop | Target | Qty | Result(Rs) | Score | Notes
 2. Trade like it is real money - same 2-trade cap, same daily limits.
 3. Do at least 30-50 trades before judging the strategy. One week = nothing.
 4. Watch two numbers on the dashboard:
        Win Rate       - % of trades that hit target
        Profit Factor  - gross profit / gross loss (above 1.5 is good)
 5. If win rate is low, RAISE "Minimum score to trade" to 120 (needs all 6
    filters) -> fewer trades but higher quality.
 6. Review your journal weekly. Look for patterns: which sector, which time,
    which score range wins most.
